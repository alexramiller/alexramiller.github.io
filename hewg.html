Bay Area Site Distribution Map

This map displays an analysis of the distribution of housing unit capacity across Bay Area jurisdictions with certified 6th Cycle Housing Elements.*

A "Fair Housing Land Use Score" is calculated for each jurisdiction, ranging from a score of -1 to +1.

  • A score of -1 indicates that a jurisdiction has located all of its housing capacity in its lowest-income neighborhood (for "median income" layers) or its least white neighborhood (for "% white" layers).
  • A score of +1 indicates that a jurisdiction has located all of its housing capacity in its highest-income neighborhood or its most white neighborhood.
  • A score of 0 indicates that housing capacity is relatively evenly distributed by income or race.

These scores are calculated for four different combinations of variables:

  • Median Income vs. Total Units: The distribution of housing unit capacity compared with neighborhood income.
  • Median Income vs. Low-Income: The distribution of sites specifically reserved for new low-income housing units compared with neighborhood income.
  • % White vs. Total Units: The distribution of housing unit capacity compared with the share of a neighborhood that is white.
  • % White vs. Low-Income: The distribution of sites specifically reserved for new low-income housing units compared with the share of a neighborhood that is white.

This map also includes the following additional layers:

  • Tract Median Income: Distribution of neighborhoods by income
  • Tract % White: Distribution of neighborhoods by % white
  • Total Units: Approximate location of housing sites, characterized by total number of proposed housing units
  • Low-Income Units: Approximate location of sites specifically for low-income housing units

*San Francisco is also not included due to data comparability issues.
