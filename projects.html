Housing Precarity Risk Model

Urban Displacement Project
2021

The Housing Precarity Risk Model (HPRM) provides a national analysis of housing precarity, identifying vulnerable neighborhoods that are in particularly urgent need of assistance and resources from local, state, and federal agencies. Precarity is measured through a combination of pre-pandemic concentrations of low-income renters, low-income displacement risk, eviction lockout risk, unemployment, and Covid-19 infections.

The State of Evictions: Results from the University of Washington Evictions Project

The Evictions Study
2019

This report presents preliminary findings from data collected from scraped court records in Washington State, demonstrating the volumes of eviction filings in different counties, and pointing to the disparities in eviction risk by race and gender for households in the Seattle metropolitan area. The report is accompanied by an interactive dashboard built with R Shiny.
